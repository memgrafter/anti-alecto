### TL;DR
IDC forecasts that worldwide smartphone shipments will fall 12.9% in 2026 to 1.1 billion units because of a severe memory supply shortage, with low-end Android vendors hit hardest and Apple/Samsung likely to gain share.

### Key Quote
“there is no return to business as usual for vendors and consumers.”

### Summary
- **What happened**
  - IDC released a February 26, 2026 forecast saying worldwide smartphone shipments will decline **12.9% YoY in 2026** to **1.1 billion units**.
  - IDC says this would be the **lowest annual shipment volume in more than a decade**.
  - The forecast was revised sharply downward from IDC’s **November forecast** because of an intensifying **memory shortage crisis**.

- **Core argument**
  - IDC describes the issue as a **structural reset**, not a temporary slowdown.
  - The shortage is expected to reshape:
    - the **total addressable market (TAM)**
    - the **vendor landscape**
    - the **product mix**
  - Rising memory/component costs are expected to compress margins, especially for **low-end Android vendors**.

- **Who is affected**
  - **Most vulnerable:** smaller vendors and Android brands concentrated at the low end of the market.
  - **Better positioned:** **Apple and Samsung**, which IDC says may weather the crisis more easily and possibly **expand market share**.
  - IDC expects **consolidation** as smaller players exit.

- **Pricing and market mix**
  - IDC projects smartphone **ASP** will rise **14%** to a record **$523** in 2026.
  - The **sub-$100 segment** is forecast at **171 million devices** and described as **permanently uneconomical**.
  - IDC says memory prices may stabilize by **mid-2027**, but are unlikely to return to prior levels.

- **Regional outlook**
  - Markets with more low-end phones are expected to suffer most.
  - Forecast declines:
    - **Middle East & Africa:** **-20.6% YoY**
    - **China:** **-10.5% YoY**
    - **Asia Pacific excluding Japan and China:** **-13.1% YoY**

- **Recovery outlook**
  - IDC expects the crisis to begin stabilizing by **mid-2027**.
  - Forecasted rebound:
    - **2027:** **+2%**
    - **2028:** **+5.2% YoY**

    - corporate “About IDC” section
    - trademark notice
