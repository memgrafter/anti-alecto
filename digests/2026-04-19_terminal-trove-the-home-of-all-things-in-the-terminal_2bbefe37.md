### TL;DR
Terminal Trove is a curated directory for terminal-based tools and TUIs, with browsing by category, language, new additions, and a featured “tool of the week.”

### Key Quote
“Find your next TUI tool at Terminal Trove”

### Summary
- Terminal Trove presents itself as “The $HOME of all things in the terminal,” focused on discovering terminal utilities and TUI apps.
- Main entry points on the homepage:
  - **Explore All Tools**
  - **Random tool**
  - **Browse by Categories**
  - **Tool of the Week**
  - **Browse Newly Added Tools**
  - **Browse by Language**
- The site appears to be a directory / catalog rather than a single app or tutorial.
- Featured or recently listed tools shown on the homepage include:
  - **netwatch** — “Real time network diagnostics in your terminal.”
  - **artui** — “Read and track recent arXiv papers with a TUI.”
  - **unifly** — “A CLI/TUI for UniFi network controller management.”
  - **sheets** — “Minimal terminal based spreadsheet and CSV viewer.”
  - **lazyenv** — “A TUI tool for managing multiple .env files in the terminal.”
  - **rustlens** — “A blazing-fast Rust code inspector for the terminal.”
- Language browsing options listed:
  - **Rust, Python, C, Haskell, Golang, JS, C++, Shell**
- The homepage also includes sponsorship messaging:
  - “Weekly sponsorship slots now available. Book in under 5 mins →”
  - A sponsored install prompt for **CodeRabbit CLI**
- The repeated tool cards suggest the homepage is optimized for discovery and browsing rather than deep documentation.
