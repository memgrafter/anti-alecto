### TL;DR
Anthropic announced that Claude subscribers will get new weekly rate limits in late August, saying the change affects under 5% of users and is meant to curb 24/7 heavy usage, account sharing, and service strain.

### Key Quote
“We’re committed to supporting long-running use cases through other options in the future, but until then, weekly limits will help us maintain reliable service for everyone.”

### Summary
- **What happened**
  - An official Anthropic post in r/ClaudeAI announced **weekly rate limits for Claude subscription customers**.
  - Timing: **“In late August”**.
  - Anthropic says the change affects **less than 5% of users** based on current usage patterns.

- **Why Anthropic says it’s doing this**
  - Some advanced users have been running Claude **continuously 24/7**.
  - Anthropic says this consumes resources far beyond typical usage.
  - One cited example: a user consumed **“tens of thousands”** in model usage on a **$200 plan**.
  - The company says the limits will:
    - make access more equitable,
    - prevent policy violations like **account sharing** and **reselling access**,
    - help maintain reliable service while reliability/performance issues are being fixed.

- **What the post says about plans**
  - **Pro and Max** plans are described as offering generous access, but not unlimited.
  - **Max 20x** subscribers can buy **additional usage at standard API rates** if needed.
  - Anthropic says it is still working on other options for long-running use cases in the future.

- **How the thread reacted**
  - Large portions of the discussion focused on whether this is really about abuse vs. a broader monetization move.
  - Common reaction themes:
    - **Demand for transparency**: users want a visible usage indicator, per-model limits, and time windows.
    - **Fairness concerns**: many objected to what they called “collective punishment” for normal users.
    - **Skepticism about the 5% claim**: several commenters mocked or doubted it, suggesting the affected group may be larger.
    - **Disagreement over “abuse”**: some argued 24/7 use is clearly abusive or against TOS; others said paying customers should be able to use the service heavily within the rules.
    - **Limits confusion**: users were unsure whether the new limit is per day, per week, or based on some opaque session model.
    - **Business motive suspicions**: some commenters believed the real goal is to push upgrades or reduce load/costs, not just stop bad actors.

- **Notable subdiscussion points**
  - Several users asked why Anthropic doesn’t simply **ban abusers** instead of reducing limits for everyone.
  - Others said a weekly limit is actually more practical than daily limits for bursty work.
  - A recurring complaint: users do not know **how much of their allowance they’ve used**, making it hard to plan work.
  - The mod team posted that comments should go to a **new Usage Limits Megathread**, and the thread was locked while remaining visible.
