#### What the guide says about eGPU hardware
- An Intel-certified Thunderbolt 3 eGPU enclosure typically includes:
  - **Thunderbolt controller**  
    - Alpine Ridge is the older popular family
    - Titan Ridge is newer and works with USB-C systems too
  - **USB-C controller**
    - Texas Instruments TPS65983 is highlighted for compatibility and power delivery
  - **Power supply**
    - either internal PSU or external AC adapter
  - **Enclosure/case**
- It explains common enclosure features:
  - **Power Delivery**: typically 15W downstream, up to 100W upstream charging
  - **Expansion I/O**: Ethernet, USB, SATA, audio, etc.
  - **Daisy chaining**: multiple Thunderbolt devices over one cable

#### Performance guidance
- Real-world eGPU performance depends on:
  - CPU architecture
  - PCIe lane width
  - cooling
  - cable quality
  - application optimization
- The page claims gaming performance is often **up to about 80% of desktop-level**
- It recommends:
  - **quad-core CPUs**
  - **Intel 8th gen or newer** for better results
  - **x4 PCIe over Thunderbolt** for best bandwidth
- It notes eGPUs generally do better in **compute workloads** than in gaming.

#### Setup / OS support notes
- **Windows**: most mature support, usually plug-and-play
- **Linux**: good support, but display and driver setup may still need work
- **macOS**:
  - improved eGFX support
  - **AMD is the only choice starting with Mojave 10.14**
  - Boot Camp lacks official eGPU support
- The guide references community tools and methods such as:
  - **kryptonite**
  - **Purge-Wrangler**
  - **automate-eGPU EFI**

#### Notes and caveats
- Some footnotes warn that:
  - USB-based enclosure I/O ports can be flaky under load
  - Titan Ridge can provide about **10% more PCIe bandwidth** than older Alpine Ridge, but with little effect on gaming FPS
  - flex-ATX PSUs can be noisy because of small high-RPM fans
  - Thunderbolt 2/1 Macs can use better TB3 enclosures via the **Apple TB3-to-TB2 adapter**
  - USB4 systems may require stricter certified cables
