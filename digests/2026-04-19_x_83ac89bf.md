### TL;DR
Sam Altman says OpenAI is amending its deal with the Department of War to explicitly ban intentional domestic surveillance of U.S. persons, limit use by intelligence agencies like the NSA, and emphasize democratic oversight, while also acknowledging the announcement was rushed and poorly communicated.

### Key Quote
“it should be the government making the key decisions about society.”

### Summary
- This is a repost of an internal post explaining changes OpenAI is making in its agreement with the Department of War.
- The main contractual addition is explicit language saying the AI system will **not be intentionally used for domestic surveillance of U.S. persons and nationals**, consistent with laws including:
  - the **Fourth Amendment**
  - the **National Security Act of 1947**
  - the **FISA Act of 1978**
- The post further clarifies that this prohibition includes:
  - deliberate tracking, surveillance, or monitoring of U.S. persons/nationals
  - use of **commercially acquired personal or identifiable information**
- Altman says this clarification is important for protecting Americans’ civil liberties and that the teams worked together to make the principle clear.
- He says the Department also affirmed OpenAI’s services will **not be used by Department of War intelligence agencies** such as the NSA unless there is a later contract modification.
- He frames OpenAI’s role as participating in **democratic processes**, not making ultimate societal decisions itself.
- He also states that if he were given an order he believed unconstitutional, he would “rather go to jail than follow it.”
- He adds that some technologies are not ready yet and that safety tradeoffs are still not fully understood, so OpenAI will proceed carefully with technical safeguards.
- He acknowledges a mistake: the team **should not have rushed the Friday announcement**, which made the effort look “opportunistic and sloppy.”
- He says this was intended to de-escalate a worse situation and that it was a learning experience for future high-stakes decisions.
- He says he reiterated over the weekend that **Anthropic should not be designated as a SCR** and hopes the DoW gives Anthropic the same terms OpenAI agreed to.
- The post ends by saying OpenAI will host an **All Hands the next morning** to answer more questions.
