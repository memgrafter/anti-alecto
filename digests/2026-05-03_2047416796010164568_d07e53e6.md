### TL;DR
dex argues that YC’s GStack idea is directionally good but technically inefficient, saying its long markdown-based skills waste instructions/tokens and should be broken into smaller, more focused components with better progressive disclosure.

### Key Quote
“it could be SO SO SOOOO much better on the execution side”

### Summary
- **Post author:** `@dexhorthy` (`dex`)
- **Context:** A response to YC’s promotion of **GStack**, an open-source toolkit by YC President/CEO `@garrytan` that turns Claude Code into an “AI engineering team” with skills for office hours, design, code review, QA, and browser testing.
- **Main stance:**
  - Dex is **not opposed** to the goals of GStack or to YC promoting it as a lesson in “use skills and import other people’s knowledge.”
  - The critique is **technical**, focused on how transformer attention/instruction handling is being used.

- **Thread topics:**
  - **Instruction budget / token waste**
    - Many GStack commands exceed **200 instructions**, which likely reduces adherence.
    - Dex suggests routing to **smaller, more focused skills** instead.
  - **Progressive disclosure problems**
    - Many skills include long setup/init/upgrade preambles.
    - Long inline bash scripts are reread every time instead of being hidden until needed.
  - **State management in markdown**
    - Inline bash/prompting is used to manage state and version updates.
    - Dex calls this “clever” and “almost novel,” but still inefficient.
  - **Attention allocation**
    - The concern is that too much context is spent on boilerplate and noise rather than the user’s actual problem and context.
  - **Overall assessment of YC’s approach**
    - Dex is broadly bullish on “the YC way” and the idea of packaging knowledge for others.
    - But he thinks the execution could be “SO SO SOOOO much better.”

- **Referenced prior discussion:**
  - Dex mentions he tried to ping `@garrytan` with feedback.
  - He also references a Monday talk by `@aiDotEngineer` on the subject.
  - He cites `@davis7` for the observation that this is like building large complex programs in markdown.

- **Quoted YC post being replied to:**
  - YC describes GStack as an open-source toolkit that turns Claude Code into an AI engineering team, with skills for multiple engineering tasks.
