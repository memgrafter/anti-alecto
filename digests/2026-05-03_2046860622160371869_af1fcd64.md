### TL;DR
Aakash Gupta argues that Karpathy’s “small reasoning model + external memory” idea is plausible because frontier LLMs waste most parameters memorizing noisy pretraining data, so cleaner data and retrieval may beat ever-larger monolithic models.

### Key Quote
“Karpathy's proposal is to separate the two.”

### Summary
- **Main thesis:** The post claims that large frontier models are inefficient because they use enormous parameter counts to absorb noisy, low-signal internet data, and that a smaller “cognitive core” plus retrieval could outperform much larger models.
- **Core argument:**
  - Karpathy reportedly told Dwarkesh that a **1 billion parameter model** trained on **clean data** could match the intelligence of today’s **1.8 trillion parameter frontier model**.
  - The author frames this as a **compression problem**: current pretraining data is described as mostly noise, so many weights are spent memorizing junk rather than supporting reasoning.
  - The post cites an estimate that **Llama 3 compresses at about 0.07 bits per token**, while structured English carries around **1.5 bits per token** of real information.
  - That comparison is used to argue that a trillion-parameter model is like a **“roughly 5% resolution image of the internet”**—mostly overhead, not useful capability.
- **Proposed solution:**
  - Build a **small reasoning model** that contains the algorithms for problem-solving and logic.
  - Use **external memory / retrieval** for facts and encyclopedic knowledge instead of storing everything inside the weights.
  - The claim is that a **1B reasoner + retrieval** could beat a huge model trying to do both reasoning and memorization.
- **Evidence cited in the post:**
  - The author says current trends support this direction:
    - **GPT-4o** is described as roughly **200B parameters** and better than the original **1.8T GPT-4**.
    - **Inference costs for GPT-3.5-level performance** supposedly fell **280x from 2022 to 2024**, mainly due to smaller, cleaner, better-architected models.
- **Implication / takeaway:**
  - The post concludes that the key bottleneck is **data quality**, not just model size.
  - The companies most likely to win the next phase are framed as those that know **what to train on and what to discard**.
