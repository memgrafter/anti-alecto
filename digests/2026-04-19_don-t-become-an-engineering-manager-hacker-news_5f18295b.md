- **Top comment (verbatim):** “The document is comparing salaries of staff engineers, and EM's. In my experience staff engineer positions are even rarer then EM positions.”

- **Thread topics:**
  - Whether becoming an engineering manager is really “moving away from tech”
  - Staff engineer vs engineering manager availability, rarity, and compensation
  - How much job titles mean across companies and industries
  - Whether AI changes the desirability of IC vs management tracks
  - How titles map to levels, promotions, and hiring decisions

- **Main claim being debated from the linked essay:**
  - The essay argues against becoming an engineering manager, framing it as a bad career move compared with staying technical.
  - Commenters push back that this is overly absolute, and that the real decision depends on company type, region, title ladder, and whether the person wants management work.

- **Key reply clusters:**
  - **Titles are company-specific / not portable:**
    - Multiple commenters say “senior,” “staff,” “principal,” and “EM” mean very different things at different companies.
    - Several note that titles matter most within a single org for comp and performance reviews, but are weak signals across companies.
  - **Management vs IC is a real split, but not a universal downgrade:**
    - Some agree EM is a different skillset and that people promoted into it can become miserable if they wanted to stay technical.
    - Others argue EMs are still steeped in tech; the difference is the kind of technical work and the amount of people/process responsibility.
  - **Industry and region matter:**
    - One subthread argues the post is less relevant in Western Europe because traditional industries make management progression more important for SWE career safety.
    - Another notes enterprise/contract shops often use different ladders than Big Tech-style ladders.
  - **AI anxiety vs career-path argument:**
    - One commenter says the real reason not to become an EM “in 2026” is AI making jobs harder.
    - Others reject that as a blanket rule or say technical people need to keep learning deeply regardless of title.
  - **Leveling and compensation discussions:**
    - Commenters compare common ladders like L3 junior, L4 intermediate, L5 senior, L6 staff, L7 senior staff, L8 principal, etc.
    - A recurring point is that titles are often used as shorthand for compensation bands, promotion rubrics, and external leveling comparisons, even if the labels are inconsistent.
  - **Career trajectory / “terminal” roles:**
    - Some discuss EM, Senior, or VP as “terminal” or acceptable endpoints where many people stop advancing.
    - Others push back that “terminal role” ideas are company-specific or copied from Big Tech / military-style rank systems.
  - **Hiring manager skepticism:**
    - Several commenters say they ignore titles and instead look for scope, impact, concrete accomplishments, and measurable business results.
    - Some state that impressive titles from tiny startups often collapse to much lower levels at larger companies.

- **Notable disagreement:**
  - Whether titles are effectively meaningless:
    - Some say titles are arbitrary and barely portable.
    - Others say titles still matter for comp, reviews, and external leveling, especially in large companies with known ladders.
  - Whether becoming an EM is “moving away from tech”:
    - Some reject that framing entirely.
    - Others accept it as a real shift in work style, even if not a shift away from technical decisions.
