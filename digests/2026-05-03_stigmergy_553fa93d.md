### TL;DR
Stigmergy is the idea that agents coordinate indirectly by leaving traces in the environment, and this Wikipedia article explains how that mechanism shows up in insects, bacteria, computer science, wikis, and proposed social systems.

### Key Quote
“Stimulation of workers by the performance they have achieved.”

### Summary
- **Definition**
  - Stigmergy is a mechanism of **indirect coordination through the environment** between agents or actions.
  - A trace left by one action stimulates a later action by the same or a different agent.
  - It is a form of **self-organization** that can produce complex structures without planning, central control, or direct communication.
  - The concept is presented as a way simple agents can offload memory and computation to the environment.

- **History and terminology**
  - The term was introduced by **Pierre-Paul Grassé** in **1959** to describe termite behavior.
  - Grassé defined it as: “Stimulation of workers by the performance they have achieved.”
  - The word comes from Greek **stigma** (“mark, sign”) and **ergon** (“work, action”).
  - A related broader term, **sematectonic communication**, was later coined by **E. O. Wilson** for non-work-specific incitement.
  - The article says stigmergy is now a key concept in **swarm intelligence**.

- **Non-human organisms**
  - **Ants** use pheromone trails as external traces that guide others to food, forming efficient trail networks.
  - These trails function as a **shared external memory** for the colony.
  - In computer science, this inspired **ant colony optimization**, which uses “virtual pheromones” to search for solutions.
  - The article also notes use in **artificial neural networks** as computational memory, citing **Federico Galatolo** and claiming stigmergic memory can match the performance of more established architectures like **LSTM**.
  - **Termites** build nests through decentralized local rules: they place pheromone-infused mudballs, and larger heaps become more attractive, creating positive feedback that forms pillars, arches, tunnels, and chambers.
  - **Bacteria**, especially **myxobacteria**, also show stigmergy-like collective behavior through signaling, aggregation, and fruiting body formation.
  - Similar life cycles are noted in **cellular slime molds**.

- **Human behavior and systems**
  - The concept has been proposed as a model for **robotics**, **multi-agent systems**, **computer networks**, and **online communities**.
  - **Wikipedia** and open source projects like the **FreeBSD kernel** are presented as stigmergic systems: people modify local parts of a shared environment, and the accumulated structure guides later contributions.
  - The article extends the idea to **building design**, where many specialists coordinate through distributed work practices.

- **Human social movements and politics**
  - Open source and open content projects are described as having disrupted proprietary business models, with **Wikipedia** specifically noted as a threat to **Britannica**.
  - Researchers are said to study open source as a model for **peer production** and **gift economy** growth.
  - **Heather Marsh** is cited as proposing a more collaborative “stigmergic society” that would replace competition with idea- and action-driven governance.
  - The quoted vision emphasizes that ideas can be proposed and pursued without permission or consensus.
  - The **Hong Kong Umbrella Movement (2014)** is mentioned as a context where some people recommended stigmergy as a way forward.
