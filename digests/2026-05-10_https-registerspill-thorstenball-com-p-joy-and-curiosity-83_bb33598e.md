### TL;DR
Thorsten Ball’s **Joy & Curiosity #83** is a curated weekly link roundup mixing AI, software engineering, and “good writing” finds, with a strong theme that technological progress is unpredictable but can still “go well” even when it doesn’t go as intended.

### Key Quote
> “He did not create a world that went as he wanted, but he created a world that went well.”

### Summary
- **What this is:** a weekly newsletter post from Thorsten Ball (Apr 26, 2026) collecting interesting, joyful, and thought-provoking links from the previous week.
- **Core theme:** technological change is accelerating, predictions are unreliable, and second-order effects matter more than people expect.
  - The opening uses examples like:
    - trains/bicycles contributing indirectly to women’s liberation
    - the Haber-Bosch process enabling both synthetic fertilizer and, during wartime, explosives/gas warfare
  - Main point: a technology can be intended for one thing, yet produce radically different downstream outcomes.

- **AI / model ecosystem / tooling:**
  - Notes that **Amp’s smart mode now uses Opus 4.7**, and Ball says he finds it a great model.
  - Mentions a workflow where **smart mode plans** and **deep mode reviews**.
  - Recommends a talk/interview on **tokenomics** with **Dylan Patel**, especially for understanding what “compute constrained” means.
  - Comments on **Cursor’s partnership with SpaceX** and says he disagrees with the idea that “model and harness have to go hand in hand.”
  - Mentions **Claude 3.5’s tool-call behavior** as a key inflection point for agents and says that helped motivate both Amp and Claude Code.
  - Highlights **ChatGPT Images 2.0** as mind-blowing, especially the ability to reconstruct screenshots.

- **Software engineering / professional practice:**
  - Strongly recommends **Mike Acton’s “Expectations of Professional Software Engineers”** as a real look at software engineering in teams/businesses.
  - Says it still applies in the age of AI, maybe even more so.
  - Links **Richard Hamming’s “You and Your Research”** and says the transcript/talk is worth watching or reading.
  - Cites **Bill Hader on feedback** and **Orson Scott Card** to make the point that people are often right about problems but wrong about fixes.

- **Interesting pieces and predictions about the AI economy:**
  - **“What will be scarce?”** asks whether economics becomes irrelevant under abundant AI.
  - Ball highlights the idea that the economy won’t collapse; it will shift toward things technology can’t make cheap.
  - Specifically, durable jobs will be in the **relational sector**, where human involvement is part of the product.
  - Quote-worthy takeaway: you don’t need to be Picasso; you need to be the person whose involvement makes the product feel personal.

- **Markets, startup valuation, and business commentary:**
  - **“Liar’s Valuation”**: mentions being newly aware of tiered investment rounds and the practice of using heavy discounts early, then reporting later bookings as ARR.
  - References **Google’s planned up-to-$60B investment in Anthropic** as “bonkers numbers.”
  - Mentions **Elad Gil’s thoughts on the AI frontier**, including distributed IPOs, compute constraints, hidden layoffs, and the importance of environment/prompting/brand.
  - Highlights **Maggie Appleton’s “One Developer, Two Dozen Agents, Zero Alignment”** and says it resembles the future Ball sees.

- **Writing / culture / “joy” finds:**
  - Praises **Justin Jackson’s “It’s cool to care”** for defending sincerity and enthusiasm.
  - Recommends **Adam Mastroianni on capital-S science**, with the line that “Nothing ever dies. It merely becomes embarrassing.”
  - Notes **Brandolini’s Law** and recalls personally meeting Brandolini at a Ruby conference in 2015.
  - Quotes a Sky Sports line on pressure: “Pressure is a privilege.”
  - Mentions **Josh Kushner** on adversity as preparation.
  - Says **I Found It: The Best Free Restaurant Bread in America** is fantastic and tells an anecdote about Germans and French people arguing over whose bread is better, ending with a joke that Australian and French bread is bad.

- **Product/design/tools worth checking out:**
  - **large-type.com**: a very large text display utility, useful for showing things like Wi‑Fi passwords.
  - **exe.dev** raised a Series A and wants to build a cloud developers enjoy using, inspired by early Heroku.
  - **chat jimmy**: he says it exists and encourages trying it.
  - Mentions **Jeff Geerling’s post on new 10 GbE USB adapters** as the kind of hardware writeup he could read constantly.
  - Mentions **an annotated Unicode map** as something he wants more of.
  - Notes **Fabric is harder than steel** as a surprisingly interesting materials/engineering piece.

- **Apple / leadership / craftsmanship:**
  - Links **Stratechery’s “Tim Cook’s Impeccable Timing”** and says he learned Cook was behind Apple’s China manufacturing shift.
  - Reflects on Cook as the CEO during the **M1 chip** era, which Ball sees as a remarkable engineering achievement.
  - Quotes **John Ternus** describing obsessive attention to detail at a supplier facility, including counting screw grooves, as evidence of Apple’s craftsmanship culture.
