### TL;DR
Alex Volkov describes an unusual “Ralphton” hackathon in San Francisco where participants were penalized for touching their computers, wrote specs while code auto-ran, and connected a live SF event to a parallel Seoul-originated community hackathon.

### Key Quote
“Touch your computer, lose points‼️ Humans write specs, and socialize while the loop runs, and auto writes code.”

### Summary
- Alex Volkov (@altryne) says a “weirdest hackathon” happened the day before in San Francisco.
- The central rule of the event: **“Touch your computer, lose points”** — participants were expected to write specs and socialize while an automated loop handled code generation.
- The event was hosted at the **@wandb office in SF**; Volkov says he helped support the hosting and even “woke up at 3am” to fly down there.
- He frames the hackathon as part of **Ralphton**, which he says:
  - first originated in **Seoul, South Korea**
  - involved hackers writing **Ralph specs**, going to sleep, and waking up to working projects
  - was brought to SF by **@bongbonggg97, @eatnugkrap, @realsigridjin**, and around **10 other team members**
  - included some organizers flying from Seoul to San Francisco
- He says the SF event was organized after **@RayFernando1337** posted on X the previous Tuesday asking for a venue to host a hackathon sponsored by **@OpenAIDevs** because they had run out of space in SF.
- Volkov says the **SF Ralphton ended when the Korean one started**, and they sent **@GeoffreyHuntley** to Seoul.
- He mentions talks by **@romainhuet** and **@mo_tiwari** around **5:30 PM**.
- The thread also says they did a **nearly 4-hour livestream** on **@RayFernando1337’s YouTube channel**, and used it to talk about **@wandb** and **@CoreWeave** while meeting “incredible folks building cool things.”
