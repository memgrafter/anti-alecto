### TL;DR
Dev Shah argues that domain-specific fine-tuned 35B models trained on proprietary company data will matter more than ever-large frontier models, because AI is shifting from centralized “general intelligence” to widely distributed, specialized intelligence.

### Key Quote
“intelligence is moving from general and centralized to specific and everywhere.”

### Summary
- **Main claim:** You don’t always need the biggest frontier model if a smaller, fine-tuned model has deeper knowledge of your specific domain.
- **Core example:** A “fine-tuned 35B model” can know “everything about your domain” in a way that a general model like “gpt-5.4” or “opus-4.6” never will.
- **Broader thesis:** AI is becoming:
  - **more specific** rather than universally general,
  - **more distributed** rather than centralized in a few top labs,
  - **more company-local** as every organization with proprietary data can become “its own little AI lab.”
- **Scale argument:** If “a thousand little AI labs” each fine-tune on private data, the total intelligence created could exceed any single centralized lab’s output, even if it is fragmented across many organizations.
- **Operational prediction:** Fine-tuning will become a **commodity operation**.
- **Infrastructure prediction:** An “intelligent openrouter” — implied to mean a routing layer that can intelligently select among models — will become very valuable.
