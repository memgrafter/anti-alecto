### TL;DR
Dynomight’s September 2025 “random” post ties together carbon-plated marathon shoes, the supercontinent cycle and Pangea Ultima, and Ramesses III’s Sea Peoples inscription to make a few well-understood facts feel newly strange.

### Key Quote
“there shouldn’t be any simple technology that would make humans dramatically smarter, since if there was, then evolution would have already found it.”

### Summary
- Short essay made of three standalone “random, well-understood topics.”
- **Marathons / carbon-fiber shoes**
  - Notes that elite marathoners can now finish in just over 2 hours.
  - Gives the speed intuition: about **21.1 km/h (13.1 mph)**, roughly **1 mile in 4:35**, repeated **26.2 times**.
  - Explains that running efficiency comes partly from the body’s springs in the **hips, knees, ankles, and metatarsophalangeal (MTP) joints**.
  - Claims the MTP joints waste energy because they’re supported by smaller muscles/tendons than the other major joints.
  - Says newer shoes use **foam + carbon-fiber plates** to mimic the MTP joint’s spring function and return more energy.
  - Cites a meta-analysis: about **2.7% lower oxygen consumption** and **~2.18% faster marathon times**.
  - Uses this as a partial test of the **Algernon argument**: evolution may leave only “steep slopes,” not easy dramatic upgrades.
- **Pangea / supercontinent cycle**
  - Recounts that **Pangea** broke up around the start of the **Jurassic, ~200 million years ago**.
  - Argues Earth likely cycles between **supercontinents** and dispersed continents rather than staying assembled for most of its 4.5-billion-year history.
  - Explains assembly: denser oceanic plates sink; **land chunks collide, crumple into mountains, and stick together**.
  - Explains breakup: thicker, lighter continents act like an insulating blanket, trapping heat until rising mantle heat helps **rip the supercontinent apart** after tens of millions of years.
  - Mentions the predicted next supercontinent, **“Pangea Ultima,”** in about **250 million years**.
  - Notes two grim future factors: the sun will be about **2.3% brighter**, and Pangea Ultima may drive atmospheric **CO₂** high enough to make the planet uninhabitable for mammals.
- **Egypt and the Sea Peoples**
  - Describes the **Sea Peoples** as a poorly sourced group from “somewhere” that appeared in the eastern Mediterranean around **1200 BC** and caused destruction from **modern Turkey to Egypt**.
  - Links them to the **Late Bronze Age collapse** as either cause or symptom.
  - Highlights that Egyptians recorded the attacks in inscriptions attributed to **Pharaoh Ramesses III**.
  - Includes the inscription’s vivid claim that attackers were destroyed at the shore, with ships overturned and bodies piled up.
