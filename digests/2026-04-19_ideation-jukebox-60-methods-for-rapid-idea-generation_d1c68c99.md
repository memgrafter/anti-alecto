### Summary
- This page is a **reference guide** for rapid idea generation, titled **“Ideation Jukebox — 60+ Methods for Rapid Idea Generation.”**
- It opens with a **category map** of ideation approaches, including:
  - **Collision**
  - **Constraint**
  - **Inversion**
  - **Inventory & Remix**
  - **Destruction**
  - **Observation & Ethnography**
  - **Temporal**
  - **Emotional & Somatic**
  - **Random & Chaos**
- The page frames each category with a short guiding principle:
  - **Collision**: add external input and let it collide with existing knowledge.
  - **Constraint**: impose artificial limits to force creativity.
  - **Inversion**: reverse assumptions or ask for the opposite.
  - **Inventory & Remix**: recombine ideas already in memory.
  - **Destruction**: break apart an idea or product to expose structure and rebuild.
  - **Observation & Ethnography**: watch what real people do, not just what they say.
  - **Temporal**: shift perspective across time horizons.
  - **Emotional & Somatic**: use bodily state, mood, or movement to unlock associations.
  - **Random & Chaos**: use randomness as a pattern interrupt.
- A **decision flow** helps route a session based on context:
  - If you have **2–10 minutes**, it asks:
    - Are you **at a screen**?
    - Are you **alone**?
    - Are you **moving**?
    - What is your **energy level**?
    - Are you **mentally sharp**?
  - Example routing:
    - **High energy at a screen, alone** → **Collision**
    - **Medium energy at a screen, alone** → **Constraint**
    - **Low energy at a screen, alone** → **Random / Chaos**
    - **Walking/running** → **Somatic**
    - **Sitting/waiting and mentally sharp** → **Temporal**
    - **Sitting/waiting and not sharp** → **Inventory Remix**
    - **Not alone** → **Social Methods**
  - Every branch ends with the instruction to **capture immediately**.
- The **Capture Systems** section emphasizes low-friction capture:
  - Record stream-of-consciousness first, transcribe later or never.
  - Keep one dedicated append-only list, not heavily organized.
  - For 10-minute sessions, use a four-field template: **Problem → Who → Why Now → What’s the Wedge?**
- A **Power Combos** table shows how to chain two methods for better results, for example:
  - **Podcast tunnel → Walk without headphones**
  - **Complaint forum scan → “Weekend build” constraint**
  - **Unbundle a monolith → “What’s obvious in 5 years?”**
  - **Anger-fueled ideation → Explain to someone**
  - **AI generates 20 bad ideas → Merge with your half-ideas**
  - **“Opposite of [product]” → Watch real user struggle**
