### TL;DR
Endowment.dev describes an “Open Source Endowment” nonprofit designed to provide long-term, community-governed funding for critical OSS by investing donated principal and spending only annual returns.

### Key Quote
“Truly sustainable funding for critical OSS through a community-driven endowment”

### Summary
- **What it is:** A proposed/open nonprofit model for funding critical open source software through an endowment structure.
- **Core problem stated:**  
  - Open source infrastructure is heavily relied upon but often maintained by unpaid volunteers.
  - Unstable funding contributes to maintainer burnout and security risks.
  - The page cites major incidents such as **Log4Shell**, the **XZ Utils backdoor**, and **Heartbleed** as examples of what can happen when critical OSS is under-resourced.
- **Core solution:**  
  - Build a **community-led endowment fund** that uses donations to form permanent principal.
  - Only investment returns are spent, making funding more durable over time.
  - The concept is inspired by how **universities** have used endowments for centuries.
  - It emphasizes “open source alumni” as a donor community for sustaining the projects they depend on.
- **Who it’s for / positioning:**  
  - Says it is **trusted by open source creators, founders, executives, and investors**.
  - Frames itself as an **open nonprofit** where “everybody is welcome to join.”
- **Stated principles of the nonprofit model:**
  - **Data-driven & SMART goals** — donations should have measurable impact.
  - **Global outlook** — supports the global OSS supply chain rather than a single country/company/ecosystem.
  - **Maximum transparency** — open data, public governance, clear accountability.
  - **Inclusive & diverse governance** — community participation, with emphasis on individuals over companies.
  - **Decentralized funding** — broad funding base from many sources.
  - **Neutrality** — independent from political or corporate influence.
- **How it works:**
  - **Fundraising:** Individuals, companies, and foundations donate; funds become permanent principal.
  - **Investing:** Principal is placed in a **low-risk portfolio** with a target **~5% spend rate**, described as typical for U.S. universities.
  - **Grantmaking:** Grants are distributed using **open, data-driven inputs**, co-developed with members and the OSS community.
  - **Impact tracking:** Supported projects are tracked for accountability, and the model is continuously refined.
- **Overall pitch:**  
  - The page is essentially a mission statement and high-level framework for a sustainable funding mechanism for OSS, not a detailed implementation document.
