### TL;DR
Brown University researchers report that private equity firms acquired 574 U.S. autism therapy centers across 42 states by 2024, with most deals clustered from 2018–2022, raising concerns that financial incentives could increase care intensity and Medicaid costs.

### Key Quote
“It's all about the financial incentives,”

### Summary
- Brown University’s Center for Advancing Health Policy through Research studied the spread of private equity ownership in autism therapy centers and found a rapid expansion over the last decade.
- **Main finding:** private equity firms acquired **more than 500 autism therapy centers** nationwide, with the study identifying **574 PE-owned centers in 2024** across **42 states**.
- The acquisition wave was highly concentrated:
  - **Nearly 80%** of acquisitions happened over a **four-year span**
  - Most acquisitions occurred between **2018 and 2022**
  - The study counted **142 separate deals**
- States with the largest numbers of private equity-owned centers:
  - **California: 97**
  - **Texas: 81**
  - **Colorado: 38**
  - **Illinois: 36**
  - **Florida: 36**
- **16 states** had **one or no** private equity-owned clinics at the end of 2024.
- The researchers found investment was more common in states:
  - with **higher childhood autism prevalence**
  - with **fewer insurance coverage limits**
- A key result: states in the **top third for childhood autism prevalence** were **24% more likely** to have private equity-owned clinics than other states.
- Study author **Yashaswini Singh** says this is a potentially risky move into a sensitive area of health care, especially because many children using these services are covered by **Medicaid**, which could mean higher future state budget costs if care intensity rises.
- **Daniel Arnold** argues the concern is that private equity may push revenue-generating strategies, leading to:
  - more services than clinically necessary
  - widened disparities in access to care
- The researchers built the dataset using:
  - proprietary databases
  - public press releases
  - manual verification of archived websites
- They note ownership data is hard to track because private equity and private practices are **not required to disclose acquisitions**.
- The study was published in **JAMA Pediatrics** and is described as one of the first national assessments of private equity’s role in autism services.
- The team says the next step is to study outcomes such as:
  - therapy intensity
  - medication use
  - diagnosis age
  - length of treatment
- Funding for the study came from:
  - **National Institute on Aging (R01AG073286)**
  - **National Institute of Mental Health (R01MH132128)**
