### TL;DR
A polemical essay argues that AI-driven “side SaaS” dreams are doomed because code quality and solo craftsmanship no longer matter, and that survival in software now favors being absorbed into high-touch enterprise sales or one of the few platform-approved money paths.

### Key Quote
“Every minute you put into that thing, be it your own time or your LLM agent's, is a minute for the crows.”

### Summary
- The piece opens with the Swedish proverb **“elda för kråkorna”** (“building a fire for the crows”), using it as a metaphor for wasted effort: lighting a warm fire only to throw the doors open and let the cold in.
- Its target is the archetypal **indie developer building a side SaaS** on evenings and weekends, hoping for a modest subscription business like “a couple of hundred paying customers giving you $19.99 a month.”
- The author’s central claim is blunt: **that dream is “dead”** because every minute spent building it is “a minute for the crows” — whether the work is done by the founder or by an LLM agent.
- The essay argues that **competition is now brutally commoditized**:
  - “Every idea you’ve ever had” has already been had by someone else.
  - Those competitors are “better than you at SEO.”
  - AI boosters and “agentic engineering” hype only accelerate this saturation.
- It dismisses traditional product-quality concerns as irrelevant in 2026:
  - Mentions **TTFP** and **INP**
  - Mentions **“not putting your Supabase god-token in the client”**
  - Claims these quality metrics “don’t matter anymore”
- The author paints the web as structurally hostile to independent builders:
  - “React ate the web”
  - “Safari kneecapped it”
  - “Google stopped linking to it”
  - “walled gardens and tollbooths everywhere”
- The conclusion is pessimistic and practical: **the future of software lies in high-touch enterprise sales**, and only a small set of companies are realistically allowed to make money online.
- It warns that AI-boosting “sloptimists” are not exempt:
  - Their “bot armies” and token-burning productivity will not produce lasting value
  - The gains will mostly accrue to datacenters, GPUs, and other intermediaries
- It cites **Josh Collinsworth** approvingly with the idea that **AI boosterism is a class privilege**, reinforcing the essay’s view that the current AI wave advantages those with resources while hollowing out the rest.
