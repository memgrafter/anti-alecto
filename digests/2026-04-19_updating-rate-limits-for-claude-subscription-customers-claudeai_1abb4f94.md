### TL;DR
Reddit thread on Anthropic’s announcement that Claude subscription customers will get weekly rate limits in late August, with top commenter `u/adilp` mocking 24/7 “leaderboard” users and arguing the limits are aimed at heavy abusers rather than normal subscribers.

### Key Quote
“in late August, we're introducing weekly rate limits for Claude subscribers”

### Summary
- **Top comment (verbatim):** "where are those leaderboard guys flexing 24/7 execution just to rack up compute for no reason."
- **Top commenter:** `u/adilp`
- **Thread topics:**
  - Whether Anthropic’s “less than 5% of users” claim is believable
  - Whether weekly limits are a fair response to 24/7 heavy usage and account sharing
  - Lack of visibility into current usage/limits in Claude’s UI and CLI
  - Whether the change is real abuse prevention or a way to push users toward higher-priced plans
  - Confusion over what counts as “heavy” or abusive usage versus legitimate intensive work

- **Original post:** Anthropic says it will introduce **weekly rate limits** for Claude subscribers in **late August**.
  - The company says this affects **less than 5% of users** based on current usage patterns.
  - It argues some advanced users are running Claude **continuously 24/7**, consuming resources far beyond typical usage.
  - Anthropic cites one user who consumed **tens of thousands** in model usage on a **$200 plan**.
  - The stated goals are to:
    - make access more equitable,
    - prevent policy violations like **account sharing** and **reselling access**,
    - maintain reliable service for everyone.
  - Anthropic says **Max 20x** subscribers can buy additional usage at **standard API rates**.
  - It also acknowledges recent **reliability and performance issues** and says those are still being fixed.

- **Main reaction clusters in the comments:**
  - **Support for limiting extreme usage:** Some commenters argue 24/7 heavy users are abusing shared resources and harming normal subscribers. A few frame this as a “tragedy of the commons” or note that the resource is not unlimited.
  - **Skepticism about the 5% figure:** Many users doubt the percentage, suspecting the real impact is larger or that “5%” is being used rhetorically.
  - **Calls for transparency:** A major recurring complaint is that Claude does **not show clear usage/quota indicators** in the chat UI or code CLI, making it hard for users to know how close they are to limits.
  - **Preference for finer-grained controls:** Several commenters want:
    - visible per-model limit meters,
    - time-frame indicators,
    - daily rather than weekly reset cycles,
    - clearer warnings before throttling.
  - **Suspicion of monetization motives:** Multiple replies claim the change is really about **reducing load / saving money** or nudging users into higher tiers rather than solely stopping abuse.
  - **Ambiguity over what counts as abuse:** Some users argue that if they stay within current TOS and hit session limits through normal intensive work, they shouldn’t be treated as abusers.

- **Notable subthread themes:**
  - Whether “leaderboard users” or “24/7 execution” is actually the problem, or just a convenient scapegoat.
  - Whether Anthropic should ban specific violators instead of applying broader limits.
  - Whether weekly limits are better than daily limits because they allow bursty work patterns.
  - Whether Claude’s pricing/usage model is transparent enough for professional use.
