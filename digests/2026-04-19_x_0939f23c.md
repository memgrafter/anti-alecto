### TL;DR
Andrej Karpathy argues that “brain upload” is already becoming technically plausible in a rough, lossy form through LLM-based simulators trained on detailed interviews and personal data, rather than through literal brain scanning and simulation.

### Key Quote
“Yes it's the tractable form of brain upload.”

### Summary
- Karpathy’s main claim is that the near-term version of “brain upload” is not sci-fi-style mind scanning, but a **lossy, approximate simulation of a person** built with **LLMs** and related tools.
- He contrasts two ideas:
  - **Exotic brain upload**: scanning and simulating the brain directly
  - **Tractable brain upload**: collecting lots of personal data and using it to fine-tune an LLM that can imitate the person
- He sketches a startup concept:
  - The person spends **a few days** doing **detailed video interviews**
  - That data is then used in an **LLM fine-tuning process**
  - The result is a simulated version of the person exposed as an **API endpoint**
- He points to **HeyGen** as evidence that parts of this are already possible, especially avatar/video-based likeness simulation.
- His proposed version would go beyond appearance/voice and combine that with an LLM that has:
  - **deep knowledge**
  - **personality**
- His tone is cautious but intrigued:
  - He calls it **“trippy”**
  - He also says it is **“admittedly kind of dystopian”**
  - But he emphasizes it is **“in principle quite possible around now”**
- The post is framed as a response to another user, **@jenzhuscott**, and appears to be a short speculative argument rather than a detailed technical proposal.
