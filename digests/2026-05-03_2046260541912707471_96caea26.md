### TL;DR
Steve Yegge argues that Google’s AI adoption is uneven and politically fraught: DeepMind engineers reportedly use Claude freely while most other Googlers are forced onto internal Gemini variants, and he pushes back on Addy Osmani’s “over 40,000 SWEs use agentic coding weekly” claim as evidence of real adoption rather than box-checking.

### Key Quote
“Volume of opens isn't adoption — and "weekly" is a low bar that includes a lot of people who tried it once and went back to writing code by hand.”

### Summary
- Yegge says his earlier tweet about Google’s AI adoption drew pushback, but he then received multiple independent, anonymous outreach messages from Googlers across orgs.
- Those Googlers reportedly described:
  - Fear of being doxxed
  - Concern that Yegge was being bullied
  - Corroboration of his earlier criticism
- The picture he says emerges is a **two-tier system** inside Google:
  - **DeepMind engineers** reportedly use **Claude** as a daily tool
  - **Most other Google engineers** do not have comparable access
- He says that when equalizing access came up internally:
  - One proposed response was to **remove Claude for everyone**
  - DeepMind reportedly objected so strongly that several engineers threatened to leave
- Non-DeepMind engineers are reportedly pushed onto **internal Gemini variants** with router-style names that obscure which model is actually serving the request.
- Multiple engineers allegedly reported:
  - Regressions
  - Reliability problems
  - Enough friction that some senior people stopped using the tools
- He adds that a senior manager on a major product line reportedly raised **attrition concerns** tied to this issue.
- According to Yegge, leadership knows the gap is real, but the response has been:
  - Mandating AI usage in **OKRs** and individual expectations
  - Creating an internal **token-usage leaderboard**
- He says managers are being told conflicting things:
  - the leaderboard will **not** be used for performance reviews
  - and also that it **will** be used
- He references **Addy Osmani’s** reply on behalf of Google claiming **over 40,000 SWEs use agentic coding weekly**.
  - Yegge says he doesn’t doubt the number
  - but argues this is still **thin, box-checking usage**
  - and that weekly opens do not equal meaningful adoption
- His broader conclusion:
  - Googlers want better agentic tools
  - they are repeatedly asking for them
  - Google is **not** in a healthy or settled state on engineering AI adoption
  - the goal is to get more people using AI and agentic coding, while avoiding the illusion that outsiders are much farther ahead than they really are
