### TL;DR
Mark Cuban argues that AI-native startups will pressure incumbents into an “Innovator’s AI Dilemma,” where CEOs must choose between reinventing their companies around AI or risking displacement, shareholder backlash, and lawsuits either way.

### Key Quote
“If asking your models questions doesn’t make sense to you, you are in deep shit”

### Summary
- Cuban says entrepreneurs who understand AI are actively trying to build **AI-native companies** that can **displace incumbents** rather than compete incrementally.
- He frames the challenge for established firms as an **“Innovator’s AI Dilemma”**: if startups gain traction and can’t be acquired, incumbents face a hard strategic choice.
- The main dilemma for CEOs:
  - **Rebuild the company as AI-native**, even if that means tearing down existing structures.
  - **Avoid major change**, which could leave the company vulnerable to disruption.
- He highlights the **public-company communications problem**:
  - If CEOs radically transform the business, shareholders may sue over the stock price collapse.
  - If they do not transform it, shareholders may sue over failing to act while the company is disrupted.
- Cuban suggests many CEOs **do not yet understand AI well enough** to make these decisions intelligently.
- His practical advice is to start by **asking AI models how to move from the current business to an AI-native version with the same economics**.
- The post’s core warning is that if a leader cannot even think in terms of querying AI for strategic transformation, they are already far behind.
