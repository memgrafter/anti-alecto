### TL;DR
A videographer warns that selling used gear to Adorama can become a frustrating, slow, and error-prone process—especially if you need the item returned—after they allegedly shipped a $5,000 kit to the wrong address without confirming it.

### Key Quote
“Do NOT try to sell your used gear to Adorama, you WILL regret it.”

### Summary
- **Original poster’s experience**
  - The author had a camera kit worth around **$5,000** and wanted to sell it while switching ecosystems.
  - After **30 days of failed private selling**, they requested a quote from **Adorama** and were contacted by an employee who said they’d pay well if the gear was in good condition.
  - The author was told that if they rejected the offer or changed their mind, Adorama would **ship the gear back for free**, matching what the website reportedly says.

- **What went wrong**
  - The day after shipping the gear, the author received a **private-party offer for the asking price** and asked Adorama to **abort the evaluation** and return the kit.
  - Adorama reportedly said the evaluation would take **7–10 business days**, and the author was given a point of contact in the evaluation department.
  - That contact **never responded** to emails or calls about return shipping addresses.
  - The only response they got was from **Frank Brown**, who kept saying the gear would be shipped back, but **with no ETA**.
  - About **5 days** after requesting the return, Adorama sent a **UPS tracking number** showing the kit was shipped to the **wrong address**.
  - The author says Adorama:
    - never asked them to confirm an address,
    - never responded to multiple attempts to provide one,
    - apparently used an **old address from their customer history**,
    - and shipped the package to an address **an hour away** that they had moved from **a year earlier**.
  - After contacting Adorama and UPS, the author learned the address **could not be amended**.
  - They had to pay **$8** to hold the package for pickup at a distribution center in the distant city.

- **Author’s conclusion**
  - The post argues Adorama handled the situation carelessly and treated a **$5,000 shipment** as if it didn’t matter.
  - The author ends with a warning to others not to use Adorama for selling used gear.

- **Comment themes / community reaction**
  - **General distrust of big retailers:** Many commenters frame this as typical of large businesses running understaffed, low-communication operations.
  - **Similar Adorama complaints:** Multiple users say Adorama’s **used department** is the biggest problem, especially:
    - inflated initial quotes followed by much lower offers after inspection,
    - slow response times,
    - difficulty getting the right person to respond,
    - trouble resolving follow-up issues like missing store credit.
  - **Mixed retail experiences:** A few users say **Adorama retail in person** has been fine, and some have had positive used-gear experiences—but the overall used-buyback process is described as slow and inconsistent.
  - **Alternatives recommended:** Users mention **MPB** and **KEH** as better options for used gear trading/selling, often citing quicker turnaround and fairer pricing.
  - **Common advice:** Several commenters stress that selling to a retailer usually yields less than private sale, but the tradeoff should be convenience—not lost communication or shipping mistakes.
