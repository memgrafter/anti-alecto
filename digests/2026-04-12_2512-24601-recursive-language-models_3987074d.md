### TL;DR
Recursive Language Models (RLMs) propose an inference-time framework where an LLM can inspect, decompose, and recursively re-invoke itself on long prompts, letting it handle inputs far beyond its native context window and improve long-context task performance.

### Key Quote
“We propose Recursive Language Models (RLMs), a general inference paradigm that treats long prompts as part of an external environment and allows the LLM to programmatically examine, decompose, and recursively call itself over snippets of the prompt.”

### Summary
- **Paper type:** Research/technical arXiv preprint in computer science / AI.
- **Topic:** Extending LLM capability to **arbitrarily long prompts** via **inference-time scaling** rather than only increasing context length.
- **Core idea: Recursive Language Models (RLMs)**
  - Treat the long prompt as an **external environment**.
  - The model can:
    - **examine** parts of the prompt,
    - **decompose** the task,
    - **recursively call itself** over prompt snippets.
  - This is presented as a **general inference paradigm**, not just a training trick.
- **Claims/results**
  - RLMs can process inputs **up to two orders of magnitude beyond** the model’s context window.
  - Even on shorter prompts, RLMs **outperform vanilla frontier LLMs** and common long-context scaffolds on **four long-context tasks**, with **comparable cost**.
- **Small-scale training result**
  - The authors post-train the first **natively recursive language model**.
  - Their model, **RLM-Qwen3-8B**, is reported to:
    - outperform the base **Qwen3-8B** by **28.3% on average**,
    - and **approach vanilla GPT-5** on **three long-context tasks**.
