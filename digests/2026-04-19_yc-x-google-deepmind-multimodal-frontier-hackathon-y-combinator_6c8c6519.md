### TL;DR
YC and Google DeepMind hosted an in-person San Francisco hackathon focused on building genuinely multimodal AI apps using early access to Gemini 3.1, Lyria, and NanoBanana 2, with no travel support and an event schedule running from 9:30 AM to 9:00 PM.

### Key Quote
“Most "AI apps" are still just text boxes with extra steps.”

### Summary
- **Event name:** YC x Google DeepMind: Multimodal Frontier Hackathon
- **Hosts:** Y Combinator and the Google DeepMind team
- **Format:** “low-key, high-signal hackathon”
- **Location:** In-person in San Francisco
- **Travel support:** None
- **Status:** The event has ended

**What the event was about**
- The stated goal was to move beyond text-only AI apps and explore what becomes possible when using:
  - **native audio**
  - **real-time video**
  - **high-fidelity image generation**
- The organizers explicitly wanted projects that were not possible “six months ago.”
- They encouraged building things that go past the chatbot paradigm, such as:
  - a **real-time foley engine**
  - a **spatial video reasoning agent**
  - or “something so weird we haven't named the category yet”

**Models / tech provided**
- Participants were given early access and high-rate limits for:
  - **Gemini 3.1** — described as the latest iteration with expanded long-context reasoning and native agentic vision
  - **Lyria** — DeepMind’s specialized model for high-fidelity music and transformational audio
  - **NanoBanana 2** — described as state-of-the-art for image composition, character consistency, and sub-pixel text rendering

**People present**
- The event included members of DeepMind research and product teams who were available to help debug and brainstorm:
  - **Shubham Agrawal** — DeepMind Researcher
  - **Paige Bailey** — DeepMind Developer Experience Lead
  - **Abinaya Rajesh** — ML lead, NotebookLM & former founder
  - **Min Choi** — DeepMind Product Manager, GenAI & Gemini Long Context
  - **Naman Goyal** — DeepMind Machine Learning Engineer

**Schedule**
- **9:30 AM** — Doors open
- **10:30 AM** — Hacking begins
- **5:00 PM** — Final presentations
- **8:30 PM** — Prizes
- **9:00 PM** — Event ends
