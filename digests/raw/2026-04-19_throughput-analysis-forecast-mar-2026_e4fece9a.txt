Recommendations — Ranked by Expected Impact
1. Kill 8hr+ sessions HIGH IMPACT — estimated +60% throughput
55 sessions lasted 8+ hours and committed at 0.07/hr — 37× worse than sub-30-min sessions. These sessions have 28.9 turns on average, so they aren't "abandoned" — they're actively used but stuck in exploratory loops.
- Action: Hard-stop sessions at 2 hours. Save state (commit or WIP snapshot), start fresh.
- Mechanism: If a session hits compaction, terminate immediately. Compacted sessions run at 0.66 c/hr vs 4.14 c/hr non-compacted — that's 6× worse.
- Math: 55 sessions × 0.07 c/hr → if those hours ran at the 1-2hr bucket rate (1.79 c/hr), that's ~25× more commits per hour from the same active time.
2. Single-project focus days HIGH IMPACT — estimated +25% throughput
Days with 1-2 projects produce 0.94 commits/active hr vs 0.40 for 6+ project days. Your best day ever (Mar 17, 6.91 c/hr) was single-project.
- Action: Designate 3-4 days/week as single-project focus days. Reserve 1-2 days for multi-project maintenance.
- Why it works: Context switching between projects forces re-reading, re-orienting, and re-planning. Each switch costs 10-20 minutes of setup overhead.
- Target: Achieve at least 2 days/week with ≤2 active projects.
3. Target the <60-min session shape MEDIUM IMPACT — estimated +20% throughput
The data shows a clear efficiency curve: sub-60-min sessions commit at 2.2-2.6/hr. The 1-2 hour bucket is still good (1.79). Beyond 2 hours, throughput degrades sharply.
- Action: Design sessions with a clear commit target. "This session will ship X." When X is done, commit and close.
- Batch size: 1-3 commits per session is the sweet spot. Elite sessions average 9.3 commits in 1.6 hours — about 1 commit every 10 minutes.
- Anti-pattern: "Let me also look at Y while I'm here" — this is the #1 session expander. Save Y for the next session.
4. Shift autonomous agentic work to burst mode MEDIUM IMPACT — structural improvement
Long-running autonomous sessions (flatagents/sterling-swarm) produce significant raw output but at lower per-hour rates. Session c67e289a
produced 34 changeset commits — but took 8.1 hours.
- Action: Break autonomous work into 90-minute sprints with explicit phase checkpoints (design → implement → test → commit).
- Evidence: Session
cfb3272b
(flatagents, 11 commits in 0.79 hours = 14.0 c/hr) proves the same type of work can be done in short bursts. - Target: No autonomous session exceeds 2 hours without a commit checkpoint.
5. Protect zero-error operating mode DEFENSIVE — sustains existing gains
Zero-error sessions commit at 0.27/hr vs 0.08 for 1-3% error sessions. Errors don't just cost retry time — they break flow state and trigger exploration spirals.
- Action: Continue current reliability practices. Don't sacrifice error rate for speed.
- Leading indicator: If error rate rises above 1%, expect throughput to drop proportionally.
- Current status: Error rate at 0.06% — excellent. Protect this.
6. Exploit peak hours SCHEDULING — free throughput
Your 12 PM and 8 PM windows produce 3.7-6.3 commits/active-hr. Your 10 PM-midnight window (high volume) produces only 0.23/hr because it's dominated by long-running autonomous sessions.
- Action: Schedule high-priority commit work for 12 PM and 8 PM windows. Reserve late night for autonomous/batch work.
- Rationale: Matching high-intent work to high-throughput windows is free velocity.

The Throughput Equation
$$\text{Commits/Hr} = \text{Base Rate} \times \frac{1}{\text{Duration Factor}} \times \frac{1}{\text{Error Factor}} \times \text{Focus Multiplier}$$
Where:
- Base rate ≈ 2.4 c/hr (observed sub-60min average)
- Duration factor: 1.0 for <1hr, 1.3 for 1-2hr, 3.5 for 2-4hr, 34× for 8hr+
- Error factor: 1.0 for 0%, 1.8 for <1%, 3.4 for 1-3%
- Focus multiplier: 1.25 for 1-2 projects, 1.0 for 3-5, 0.53 for 6+
Ideal operating point: \(\text{2.4} \times \frac{1}{1.0} \times \frac{1}{1.0} \times 1.25 = \textbf{3.0 c/hr}\)
