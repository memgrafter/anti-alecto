## Original post
I’ve been experimenting with creating Claude Skills, and to make the process easier, I asked ChatGPT to generate a simple infographic as a checklist. I now use this as my standard workflow when I convert an existing template into a reusable skill. When I transform a template into a Skill, I usually work with the following two prompts — this setup has worked very reliably for me. ⸻ Prompt 1 – Reverse-Engineer the Design Template (created with support from the Claude Prompt Generator) You will be reverse-engineering a document template to extract its complete design specification. Your goal is to analyze the template methodically and document every design element so that it can be reproduced with pixel-perfect accuracy. Here is the document template to analyze: <document\_template> {{DOCUMENT\_TEMPLATE}} </document\_template> Your task is to extract and document the exact design specifications from this template. You should analyze it systematically to ensure nothing is missed. Before providing your final design specification, use <scratchpad> tags to work through your analysis methodically. In your scratchpad: \- Examine the overall layout and structure \- Identify all visual elements present \- Note patterns and consistency in design choices \- List any questions or ambiguities you notice In your analysis, extract the following design elements: Typography: \- Font families (exact names) \- Font sizes (in points, pixels, or other units) \- Font weights (light, regular, medium, bold, etc.) \- Line heights and letter spacing \- Text alignment and justification Colors: \- All colors used (provide hex codes, RGB values, or other precise specifications) \- Where each color is applied (text, backgrounds, borders, etc.) Layout & Spacing: \- Margins (top, bottom, left, right) \- Padding around elements \- Spacing between sections and elements \- Page dimensions if applicable Visual Elements: \- Logos (dimensions, placement, file format if discernible) \- Icons or graphics (size, position, style) \- Borders and dividers (thickness, style, color) \- Backgrounds (solid colors, gradients, patterns, images) Formatting & Style: \- Heading hierarchy and styles \- Paragraph styles \- List formatting (bullets, numbering) \- Table formatting if present \- Any special styling (shadows, rounded corners, etc.) Structural Elements: \- Header and footer specifications \- Column layouts \- Section divisions \- Any repeating patterns or templates Provide your final design specification inside <design\_specification> tags. Structure your specification clearly with sections for each category of design elements. Be precise and comprehensive so that someone could recreate this template exactly based on your documentation alone. ⸻ Prompt 2 – Convert the Template into a Claude Skill Use the “Skills Creator” skill and create a new skill that ensures all documents generated by the system strictly follow our template with pixel-perfect accuracy. The design details extracted from the template must be embedded into the skill and enforced as binding requirements for every generated document.
