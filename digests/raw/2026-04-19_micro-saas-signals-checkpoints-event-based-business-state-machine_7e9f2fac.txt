1. Why Events, Not Time
The problem with time-based thinking
- Variance is enormous — one founder hits $10K MRR in 3 months, another in 18. Same playbook, wildly different timelines.
- Time creates false urgency — "I should be at $5K MRR by month 6" leads to panic-driven decisions instead of signal-driven ones.
- Time creates false patience — "It's only month 2, too early to worry" when danger signals are already firing.
- Calendar milestones aren't actionable — knowing "Phase 3 starts at month 4" tells you nothing about what to do right now.
The event-based alternative
- Signals are observable — you can instrument and detect them
- Checkpoints are binary — you've either passed one or you haven't
- Transitions are causal — Signal X fires → you enter State Y → action Z is correct
- Speed-independent — works whether you reach PMF in 8 weeks or 18 months
- Danger signals fire early — bad trajectories are detected by what's happening, not how long it's been
Think of your business as emitting a continuous stream of events. Your job is to listen, classify them, and let them trigger the right state transitions.

State definitions
| State | Code | You are here when… | Primary job | Exit condition |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Idle | S0 | No active hypothesis | Generate problem hypotheses | Form a testable hypothesis |
| Searching | S1 | You have a problem hypothesis but no external signal | Find people with the problem | A stranger confirms pain unprompted |
| Validating | S2 | Strangers confirm pain; you're testing willingness to pay | Collect demand proof (money or high-cost behavior) | Demand proof threshold met |
| Building | S3 | Demand proven; constructing MVP | Ship smallest thing that solves the core loop | First user completes core action |
| Live | S4 | Product works; testing conversion | Get first paying stranger | Stranger pays without personal persuasion |
| PMF Testing | S5 | Revenue exists; testing for product-market fit | Listen for PMF signals, iterate fast | PMF checkpoint passes |
| Growing | S6 | PMF confirmed; scaling acquisition | Build repeatable growth loops | Organic growth self-sustaining |
| Scaling | S7 | Growth loops working; optimizing efficiency | Improve unit economics, reduce founder dependency | Revenue stabilizes with healthy margins |
| Mature | S8 | Stable, profitable, low-touch | Harvest or evolve | Exit signal fires |
| Danger | D | Any danger signal fires (interrupt state) | Diagnose and respond immediately | Root cause addressed or exit |
3. Signal Taxonomy
Signals are observable business events. Each has a strength rating that determines how much weight to give it in decisions.
MODERATE — medium-cost behavior (email, signup, demo request). Useful in aggregate.
WEAK — low/zero-cost behavior (likes, comments, "cool!"). Noise unless overwhelming.
ANTI-SIGNAL — looks like progress but isn't. Actively misleading.
DANGER — requires immediate response regardless of current state.
📡 Demand Signals
Fire during S1→S2→S3. Answer: "Do people want this?"
| Signal | Strength | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Stranger pre-pays / deposits | STRONG | Real demand exists — build it |
| Stranger describes your idea back to you unprompted | STRONG | Problem is real and top-of-mind |
| Stranger shares their current ugly workaround | STRONG | Active pain; they're already "hiring" a solution |
| Competitor exists with bad reviews | STRONG | Validated market, unsatisfied demand |
| 5+ people ask the same question in a forum/week | MODERATE | Recurring pain; worth investigating |
| Waitlist signup with real email | MODERATE | Interest exists; not yet commitment |
| Social media engagement on problem post | WEAK | Attention, not demand |
| Friends say "great idea!" | ANTI-SIGNAL | Politeness, not signal. Ignore completely. |
| People ask for features before the core exists | ANTI-SIGNAL | Ideation addiction — they won't pay either |
💰 Conversion Signals
Fire during S4→S5. Answer: "Will people pay?"
| Signal | Strength | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Stranger pays without personal selling | STRONG | Self-serve conversion works — PMF signal |
| Customer upgrades to higher tier unprompted | STRONG | Expansion revenue potential; value exceeds price |
| Customer pays annual upfront | STRONG | High confidence in long-term value |
| Trial→paid conversion >5% | MODERATE | Funnel is working at acceptable rate |
| User completes core action in first session | MODERATE | Activation is healthy — value delivered fast |
| Demo booked but no follow-up | WEAK | Curiosity, not intent |
| Signup but never logs in | ANTI-SIGNAL | Acquisition works, activation broken |
| High trial count but zero conversions | DANGER | Value proposition or pricing is wrong |
🔁 Retention & PMF Signals
Fire during S5→S6. Answer: "Do they keep using it?"
| Signal | Strength | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Sean Ellis test: 40%+ "very disappointed" if gone | STRONG | Canonical PMF signal |
| Unprompted referral (user brings another user) | STRONG | Organic viral loop emerging |
| Customer writes unsolicited positive review/tweet | STRONG | Emotional attachment — strong retention predictor |
| User returns daily/weekly without prompting | STRONG | Habit formed — core loop is sticky |
| Customer asks "can I do X with this too?" | MODERATE | Expanding their mental model of your tool — good |
| Low support volume per customer | MODERATE | Product is intuitive — operational leverage |
| Renewal after payment failure (active recovery) | MODERATE | Customer actively chose to stay |
| User "activates" but never returns | ANTI-SIGNAL | Activation metric is wrong, or core loop has no habit trigger |
| Month 2 cohort retention <40% | DANGER | Leaky bucket — growth won't help |
📈 Growth Signals
Fire during S6→S7. Answer: "Is this scaling?"
| Signal | Strength | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Organic signups exceed paid/manual for 4+ weeks | STRONG | Growth loop is self-sustaining |
| Content ranks page 1 for target keywords | STRONG | SEO flywheel turning — compounding distribution |
| Net revenue retention >100% | STRONG | Expansion exceeds churn — even zero new customers = growth |
| Competitor mentions you in their marketing | MODERATE | You're on their radar — market validation |
| Partnership inbound interest | MODERATE | Ecosystem value recognized |
| Signup spike from single source then flatline | ANTI-SIGNAL | One-time event, not a loop. Don't build on it. |
| Growth only while actively posting on social | ANTI-SIGNAL | Founder-dependent distribution — not scalable |
| CAC rising faster than ARPU | DANGER | Unit economics deteriorating — growth is destroying value |
⚙️ Maturity Signals
Fire during S7→S8. Answer: "Is this sustainable without me?"
| Signal | Strength | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue survives 2-week founder absence | STRONG | Business, not freelancing. You've built a machine. |
| Support handled without founder for 1+ month | STRONG | Operations are documented and delegated |
| No code deploys for 30 days, revenue stable | STRONG | Product is mature — features are right |
| Gross margin >85% sustained 6+ months | MODERATE | Cost structure is optimized |
| Monthly churn <3% sustained 6+ months | MODERATE | Product-market fit is deep — switching costs working |
| Founder is bored | MODERATE | Legitimate signal! Business is on autopilot. Time to evolve or exit. |
🏁 Exit Signals
Fire during S8→S9. Answer: "Should I sell, hold, or evolve?"
| Signal | Strength | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| Unsolicited acquisition offer | STRONG | Market values your asset — explore it |
| Opportunity cost of your time exceeds business value | STRONG | Your time is worth more elsewhere |
| Platform you depend on is declining | STRONG | Sell while asset still has value |
| Revenue flat for 12+ months despite effort | MODERATE | Market ceiling reached — milk it or sell |
| Founder motivation near zero | MODERATE | Unmotivated founder = declining product quality |
| Acquirer could 10× the product's reach | MODERATE | Strategic premium available — timing advantage |
4. Checkpoint Registry
🔍 Problem Confirmed
Transition: S1 (Searching) → S2 (Validating)
Unlocked actions:
- Create a landing page with clear value proposition
- Set up waitlist with email capture
- Begin concierge/manual validation
- Start pre-sale conversations
Anti-patterns to avoid:
- ❌ Starting to code anything
- ❌ Buying a domain and designing a logo
- ❌ Counting friend/family feedback as stranger signals
💵 Demand Proof
Transition: S2 (Validating) → S3 (Building)
Unlocked actions:
- Commit to building MVP — scope locked to validated problem only
- Choose tech stack
- Set hard ship deadline (max 8 weeks from this checkpoint)
⚡ Core Loop Complete
Transition: S3 (Building) → S4 (Live)
What "core action" means:
The single workflow that delivers the primary value. Examples:
- Form builder → user creates a form AND receives a submission
- Analytics tool → user connects data source AND sees their first dashboard
- Automation tool → user creates a rule AND it fires successfully
Unlocked actions:
- Set up billing (Stripe, Lemon Squeezy)
- Launch to waitlist / pre-sale customers
- Begin collecting NPS/feedback
🎯 First Paying Stranger
Transition: S4 (Live) → S5 (PMF Testing)
This is the most emotionally significant checkpoint but not PMF. It's proof of concept for the conversion funnel. One person paid — now the question becomes whether this repeats.
Unlocked actions:
- Instrument every step of the user journey
- Set up cohort tracking
- Begin Sean Ellis survey with every new customer
- Schedule customer calls (to understand, not to sell)
🔂 Repeatable Conversion
Transition: Still S5, but unlocks more aggressive investment
What this proves:
- Your conversion isn't a fluke or single-channel dependency
- The value proposition resonates across at least two audiences
- You can start reasoning about unit economics
Unlocked actions:
- Calculate real CAC and LTV
- Begin testing pricing changes
- Invest in the better-performing channel
🔒 Retention Lock
Transition: Still S5, but critical gate for PMF
Without this checkpoint, nothing else matters. Growth with poor retention is filling a leaky bucket. This is the most important gate in the entire system.
Unlocked actions:
- Begin spending on growth (content, ads, partnerships)
- You have permission to hire or outsource
🏆 PMF Confirmed
Transition: S5 (PMF Testing) → S6 (Growing)
This is the compound checkpoint. No single signal proves PMF — the conjunction of all four gates does. Missing any one means you're still in S5.
Unlocked actions:
- Invest aggressively in distribution
- Begin building second growth channel
- Consider raising prices
- Start annual plan option
- Create "vs competitor" content
🌀 Growth Loop Proven
Transition: Within S6 — proof of repeatable growth engine
What "organic" means:
- SEO-sourced signups (not from your own tweets)
- Referral-sourced signups
- Marketplace-sourced signups
- Direct traffic from brand search
Unlocked actions:
- Double down on the working loop
- Start systematizing (SOPs, automation, delegation)
- Consider first hire (support or content)
💎 Expansion Revenue
Transition: S6 → S7 (Scaling) when combined with CP-08
Expansion revenue is the strongest signal that your product is becoming more valuable over time. This is the magic number for micro-SaaS sustainability.
Unlocked actions:
- Build usage-based pricing tiers
- Create add-on features
- Invest in enterprise/team features
🤖 Founder Decoupled
Transition: S7 → S8 (Mature)
The ultimate lifestyle business checkpoint. Your business is a machine that runs without you. This is the moment you have optionality: hold for cash flow, sell, or start something new.
Unlocked actions:
- Explore exit options (Acquire.com listing, broker conversations)
- Start a second product
- Reduce hours to part-time maintenance
5. Danger Signals — Interrupt Protocol
Danger signal registry
| Signal ID | Signal | Observable event | Immediate response | Recovery gate |
|---|---|---|---|---|
D-01 |
Churn spike | Monthly logo churn >2× your trailing 3-month average | Pause all feature work. Call every churned customer. Categorize reasons. | Churn returns to baseline for 2 consecutive months |
D-02 |
Conversion collapse | Trial→paid drops >50% from baseline for 2+ weeks | Check: pricing page broken? Onboarding changed? Competitor launched? Review session recordings. | Conversion rate stabilizes at ≥70% of baseline |
D-03 |
Platform shock | Platform you depend on announces API change, pricing change, or competing feature | Assess impact. If existential: begin migration immediately. If not: build contingency plan within 1 week. | Dependency on platform reduced below 50% of revenue |
D-04 |
Cost explosion | Infrastructure/API costs exceed 30% of MRR | Audit costs. Implement caching/optimization. Consider usage-based pricing adjustment. | Costs return to <15% of MRR |
D-05 |
Negative viral loop | 3+ negative public reviews/posts within 2 weeks | Respond publicly and transparently. Fix root cause. Offer affected users remediation. | Sentiment recovers (no new negative signals for 4 weeks) |
D-06 |
Founder health crisis | Founder unable to work for 1+ week without business degrading | Activate runbook. This is the signal that S7→S8 (founder decoupled) hasn't been reached. | Runbook successfully handles 2-week absence |
D-07 |
Revenue regression | MRR declines for 3 consecutive months | Decompose: is it churn, contraction, or acquisition decline? Each requires different response. | Positive MRR movement for 2 consecutive months |
D-08 |
Security/data incident | Any breach, data leak, or vulnerability exploited | Contain. Notify affected users. Fix. Post-mortem. Everything else stops. | Incident fully resolved + post-mortem published |

6. Anti-Signals — Progress Illusions
Anti-signals are the most dangerous category because they feel like forward motion. They consume resources and create false confidence. Learn to recognize and actively discount them.
Anti-signal registry
| Anti-signal | What it looks like | Why it's misleading | The real signal to watch instead |
|---|---|---|---|
| Vanity signups | 1,000 signups in first week | Signups without activation are a leaky top-of-funnel. Cost to acquire was real; value is zero. | % who complete core action within 24 hours |
| Feature request volume | "Users love us — we have 200 feature requests!" | Feature requests ≠ willingness to pay more. Most requesters won't use what they asked for. | Requests that come with "I'd upgrade/pay more if…" + follow-through |
| Social proof without revenue | Viral tweet, HN front page, 500 GitHub stars | Attention is perishable. Traffic spike ≠ sustainable acquisition channel. | Paid conversions from the same source 30 days later |
| Busy-work velocity | "Shipped 12 features this month" | Features shipped is an input metric, not an output metric. Useless unless tied to activation/retention. | % of shipped features used by ≥20% of users within 2 weeks |
| Revenue from friends | "I have 5 paying customers" (all friends/family) | Friends buy from guilt/support, not value. They'll churn when politeness wears off. | Paying strangers only. Friends/family revenue = $0 in your model. |
| LTD cash injection | "Made $50K from AppSumo launch" | One-time cash ≠ MRR. LTD customers have different behavior patterns and often generate more support load per dollar. | Monthly recurring from non-LTD customers only |
| Partnership "interest" | "Three companies want to partner with us" | Corporate interest costs them nothing to express. Until there's a signed agreement or revenue, it's noise. | Revenue or users delivered through the partnership |
| Competitor validation | "A competitor just raised $5M — validates our market!" | Their raise means they'll outspend you on marketing. It validates the problem, but threatens your position. | Your retention and NRR relative to their product |
Signal vs. anti-signal distinguisher
The X-axis is the cost to the person emitting the signal. Higher-cost signals are more reliable. Free signals are noise.

9. Compound Signals & Derived Metrics
Individual signals are useful. Combinations of signals are powerful. These compound signals synthesize multiple event streams into decision-grade indicators.
Product-Market Fit Score
A composite score from 0–100 that approximates PMF strength:
$$\text{PMF Score} = 25 \cdot S_E + 25 \cdot S_R + 25 \cdot S_V + 25 \cdot S_G$$
Where:
- \(S_E\) = Sean Ellis score: \(\min\left(\frac{\text{very\_disappointed\_\%}}{40}, 1\right)\)
- \(S_R\) = Retention score: \(\min\left(\frac{\text{M2\_retention}}{60}, 1\right)\)
- \(S_V\) = Viral score: \(\min\left(\frac{\text{unprompted\_referrals}}{3}, 1\right)\)
- \(S_G\) = Growth score: \(\min\left(\frac{\text{organic\_\%\_of\_signups}}{50}, 1\right)\)
PMF Score ≥ 80 → CP-07 passed.
PMF Score 50–79 → close, keep iterating.
PMF Score < 50 → not yet. Don't invest in growth.
Business Health Index
A compound danger signal. When this drops below threshold, enter interrupt state:
$$\text{BHI} = w_1 \cdot \hat{R} + w_2 \cdot \hat{C} + w_3 \cdot \hat{E} + w_4 \cdot \hat{M}$$
Where (all normalized 0–1):
- \(\hat{R}\) = Revenue trend (MRR growth rate, positive = healthy)
- \(\hat{C}\) = Churn health: \(1 - \frac{\text{current\_churn}}{\text{danger\_threshold}}\)
- \(\hat{E}\) = Economics health: \(\min\left(\frac{\text{LTV:CAC}}{3}, 1\right)\)
- \(\hat{M}\) = Margin health: \(\min\left(\frac{\text{gross\_margin}}{85}, 1\right)\)
Weights: \(w_1 = 0.3, w_2 = 0.3, w_3 = 0.2, w_4 = 0.2\)
BHI < 0.4 → Danger state.

10. Transition Velocity & Stall Detection
Time doesn't determine state, but time spent in a state without progress signals is itself a danger signal. If no forward-transition signal has fired within the expected range, you may be stalled.
Stall detection thresholds
| State | Expected transition signal | Normal range | Stall alarm | Stall response |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| S1 (Searching) | Stranger confirms pain | 1–4 weeks | 6 weeks with no signal | Abandon niche. Try next hypothesis. |
| S2 (Validating) | Demand proof | 2–8 weeks | 10 weeks with no pre-sale/deposit | Problem may not have WTP. Revisit or pivot. |
| S3 (Building) | Core loop complete | 4–12 weeks | 14 weeks with no user testing | Scope is too large. Cut 50% of features. |
| S4 (Live) | First paying stranger | 1–8 weeks after launch | 12 weeks with no conversion | Pricing, positioning, or onboarding is broken. Major iteration needed. |
| S5 (PMF Testing) | PMF compound checkpoint | 3–12 months | 15 months without CP-07 | PMF may not exist for this product-market combination. Consider pivot or exit. |
| S6 (Growing) | Self-sustaining organic growth | 6–18 months | 24 months founder-dependent | Growth channel may be wrong. Test alternatives or accept smaller scale. |
| S7 (Scaling) | Founder decoupled | 6–24 months | 30 months still founder-dependent | Business model may require you. Consider if lifestyle business is acceptable. |
Expected signal density by state
Each state has an expected "signal density" — how frequently you should see forward-progress signals. Below the minimum threshold, you're likely stalled.
11. Signal Chains — Event Sequences That Predict Outcomes
Individual signals are events. Signal chains are sequences of events that, when observed in order, predict specific outcomes with high reliability. These are the patterns successful micro-SaaS businesses emit.
🟢 Healthy chains
Chain H1: "Organic flywheel starting"
- Content piece ranks page 1
- Signups from that content appear
- Some convert to paid
- One writes a review/tweet mentioning you
- Review drives additional signups
Prediction: self-sustaining growth within 3–6 months
Chain H2: "Expansion revenue flywheel"
- Customer hits usage limit
- Customer upgrades (unprompted)
- Customer invites team members
- Team usage drives further expansion
Prediction: NRR >100% achievable
Chain H3: "PMF crystallization"
- Support tickets shift from "how do I?" to "can I also?"
- Churn rate drops two months in a row
- First unprompted referral
- Sean Ellis survey crosses 40%
Prediction: CP-07 imminent
🔴 Danger chains
Chain D1: "Death spiral"
- Churn increases for 2 consecutive months
- Founder responds by building more features
- Features add complexity, support load increases
- Less time for growth/marketing
- Acquisition drops → net MRR declines
Intervention: STOP building. Call churned customers. Fix root cause.
Chain D2: "Platform dependency trap"
- 80%+ signups from one platform marketplace
- Platform announces competing feature
- Marketplace ranking drops
- Revenue decline with no alternative channel
Intervention: Diversify before step 2. If at step 3, diversification is urgent.
Chain D3: "Premature scaling"
- Initial launch spike (Product Hunt, HN)
- Founder invests in paid ads to sustain momentum
- Retention is <40% but masked by high acquisition
- When ad spend stops, MRR collapses
Intervention: Cut paid acquisition. Measure naked retention. Fix product first.
Chain D4: "Boiling frog"
- Churn is 6% monthly — "not great, not terrible"
- Growth compensates, so MRR is flat/slow-rising
- Founder focuses on growth, ignores churn
- Cohorts get worse, not better
- Eventually growth rate < churn rate → decline
Intervention: Track cohort retention separately. Never let "aggregate looks OK" hide deteriorating cohorts.
