When to Use What
Match your current situation to the best method categories:
All Methods
💥 1. Collision Methods
Feed external input, let it crash into what you already know. Your brain is a loaded library — these are the shaking hands that knock books off shelves.
🔒 2. Constraint Methods
Force artificial limits. Creativity sneaks through gaps that logic can't fill. Constraints kill the paradox of choice.
🔄 3. Inversion Methods
Flip, negate, reverse what exists. If everyone's going left, look right. Inversion doesn't need new info — just new angles on old info.
🗃️ 4. Inventory & Remix Methods
Shuffle and recombine what's already in your head. You have more raw material than you think — the gold is in the combinations.
💣 5. Destruction Methods
Break something, rebuild from the rubble. Decomposition reveals structure. Destruction is creative.
👁️ 6. Observation & Ethnography Methods
Watch real humans. The gap between what people say and what they do is where products live.
⏳ 7. Temporal Methods
Shift the time horizon. The present is noisy. The past and future contain signal if you look at the right resolution.
🧘 8. Emotional & Somatic Methods
Use your body and mood state, not just your prefrontal cortex. Different neurochemical states unlock different associative pathways.
🎲 10. Random & Chaos Methods
Pure pattern interrupt. Trust the noise. When your default neural pathways are grooved too deep, randomness is the only escape.
